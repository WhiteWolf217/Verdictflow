SOFTWARE AS A SERVICE (SaaS) AGREEMENT

This Software as a Service Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of
January 15, 2025 (the "Effective Date"), by and between:

CloudStack Solutions, Inc., a Delaware corporation with offices at
1500 Technology Drive, Suite 400, San Francisco, CA 94105 ("Vendor")

and

Meridian Enterprises LLC, a Texas limited liability company with offices at
2200 Commerce Boulevard, Dallas, TX 75201 ("Customer").

WHEREAS, Vendor provides a cloud-based enterprise resource planning platform
known as "CloudStack ERP" (the "Service"); and

WHEREAS, Customer desires to subscribe to and use the Service pursuant to the
terms and conditions of this Agreement;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants herein, the parties agree:


1. DEFINITIONS

1.1 "Authorized Users" means Customer's employees, contractors, and agents
authorized by Customer to access and use the Service under this Agreement.

1.2 "Customer Data" means all data, information, content, and materials that
Customer or its Authorized Users upload, transmit, or store through the Service.

1.3 "Documentation" means Vendor's standard user manuals, online help, and
technical specifications for the Service.

1.4 "Service Level Agreement" or "SLA" means the uptime and performance
commitments set forth in Exhibit A.

1.5 "Subscription Term" has the meaning set forth in Section 8.1.


2. GRANT OF LICENSE AND ACCESS

2.1 Subject to the terms of this Agreement and payment of all applicable Fees,
Vendor grants Customer a non-exclusive, non-transferable, limited right to
access and use the Service during the Subscription Term solely for Customer's
internal business operations.

2.2 Customer may provision up to 500 Authorized Users during the initial term.
Additional users may be added at Vendor's then-current per-user pricing.

2.3 Customer shall not: (a) sublicense, sell, or transfer access to the
Service; (b) reverse engineer, decompile, or disassemble the Service;
(c) use the Service in violation of applicable law; (d) interfere with or
disrupt the integrity of the Service.


3. FEES AND PAYMENT

3.1 Customer shall pay Vendor an annual subscription fee of $180,000 USD
("Base Fee"), invoiced annually in advance.

3.2 All undisputed invoices are payable within sixty (60) days of receipt
(Net-60). Late payments shall bear interest at the lesser of 1.5% per month
or the maximum rate permitted by applicable law.

3.3 Fees are exclusive of all taxes. Customer is responsible for all
applicable sales, use, value-added, and similar taxes.

3.4 Vendor may increase Fees upon renewal by providing at least thirty (30)
days' written notice prior to the start of any renewal term. Fee increases
shall not exceed 15% per renewal term.


4. SERVICE LEVELS AND SUPPORT

4.1 Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to maintain availability
of the Service in accordance with the SLA attached as Exhibit A.

4.2 Vendor targets 99.5% monthly uptime, excluding scheduled maintenance
windows. No service credits or remedies are provided for downtime.

4.3 Vendor shall provide Customer with standard email support during business
hours (9 AM - 5 PM PT, Monday through Friday). Premium 24/7 support is
available for an additional annual fee of $24,000.


5. CUSTOMER DATA AND PRIVACY

5.1 As between the parties, Customer retains all right, title, and interest
in and to Customer Data.

5.2 Vendor shall process Customer Data solely to provide the Service and in
accordance with Vendor's then-current Privacy Policy.

5.3 Upon termination, Vendor shall delete all Customer Data within ninety
(90) days. Customer may request data export in standard CSV format prior
to termination.


6. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

6.1 All work product, modifications, enhancements, derivative works, and
any inventions conceived during the performance of this Agreement, whether
or not related to Customer's business, are the sole and exclusive property
of the Vendor.

6.2 Customer is granted no rights to Vendor's intellectual property except
the limited license expressly set forth in Section 2.

6.3 Any feedback, suggestions, or ideas provided by Customer to Vendor
regarding the Service shall become the exclusive property of Vendor, and
Vendor may use such feedback for any purpose without obligation or
compensation to Customer.


7. CONFIDENTIALITY

7.1 All information disclosed by either party that is designated as
"Confidential" or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential
shall be treated as Confidential Information.

7.2 The Receiving Party's confidentiality obligations under this Section
shall survive in perpetuity and shall not expire upon termination of this
Agreement. The Receiving Party shall protect Confidential Information
using the highest degree of care.

7.3 Confidential Information may not be disclosed to any third party
without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party, with no
exceptions.


8. TERM AND TERMINATION

8.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for
an initial term of three (3) years (the "Initial Term").

8.2 This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year
terms unless either party gives written notice of non-renewal at least
seven (7) days before the end of the then-current term. Failure to cancel
shall constitute acceptance of the renewal and all applicable fee increases.

8.3 Vendor may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if
Customer fails to pay any undisputed amount within fifteen (15) days of its
due date, without any cure period.

8.4 Upon termination for any reason, Customer shall pay all outstanding Fees
for the remainder of the then-current term, regardless of the reason for
termination.


9. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

9.1 IN NO EVENT SHALL VENDOR'S TOTAL AGGREGATE LIABILITY UNDER THIS
AGREEMENT EXCEED ONE HUNDRED DOLLARS ($100.00 USD), REGARDLESS OF THE
NATURE OF THE CLAIM.

9.2 IN NO EVENT SHALL VENDOR BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL,
SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING BUT NOT LIMITED TO
LOSS OF PROFITS, DATA, BUSINESS OPPORTUNITIES, OR GOODWILL.

9.3 The limitations in this Section shall apply regardless of whether Vendor
has been advised of the possibility of such damages and notwithstanding the
failure of essential purpose of any limited remedy.


10. INDEMNIFICATION

10.1 Customer shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Vendor and its
officers, directors, employees, agents, affiliates, successors, and assigns
from and against any and all claims, losses, damages, liabilities, penalties,
costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) of any kind
whatsoever arising out of or in connection with this Agreement, Customer's
use of the Service, or any breach of this Agreement by Customer, regardless
of cause.

10.2 Vendor shall have no indemnification obligations to Customer under
this Agreement.


11. NON-COMPETE AND NON-SOLICITATION

11.1 During the term of this Agreement and for a period of two (2) years
following termination, Customer shall not, directly or indirectly, develop,
market, sell, or distribute any software product that competes with the
Service in any geographic market worldwide.

11.2 During the term and for twenty-four (24) months thereafter, neither
party shall solicit for employment any employee of the other party.


12. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

12.1 This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with
the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict of laws
principles.

12.2 Any dispute arising out of this Agreement shall be resolved exclusively
in the state or federal courts located in Wilmington, Delaware, and each
party consents to personal jurisdiction therein.


13. DATA PROCESSING

13.1 To the extent Vendor processes personal data on behalf of Customer,
the parties acknowledge that a Data Processing Agreement may be required
under applicable data protection laws, but no such agreement is currently
attached to or incorporated into this Agreement.


14. GENERAL PROVISIONS

14.1 This Agreement, together with any exhibits, constitutes the entire
agreement between the parties regarding the subject matter hereof.

14.2 Neither party may assign this Agreement without the prior written
consent of the other party, except that Vendor may assign this Agreement
in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or substantially
all of its assets.

14.3 If any provision of this Agreement is found to be unenforceable, the
remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

14.4 Failure to enforce any provision of this Agreement shall not constitute
a waiver of such provision.

14.5 All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered by
email with confirmation of receipt, or by certified mail.
