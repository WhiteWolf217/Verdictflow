CONSULTING SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Consulting Services Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of January 15, 2025, by and between:

NovaTech Solutions Inc. ("Client"), a Delaware corporation with offices at 500 Market Street, Suite 200, San Francisco, CA 94105

and

Pinnacle Advisory Group LLC ("Consultant"), a New York limited liability company with offices at 120 Broadway, Floor 35, New York, NY 10271.

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Client desires to engage Consultant to provide strategic consulting services related to cloud infrastructure migration and digital transformation; and

WHEREAS, Consultant has the expertise and resources to perform such services;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants herein, the parties agree as follows:

1. SCOPE OF SERVICES

1.1 Consultant shall provide the services described in Exhibit A ("Services"), which may be amended from time to time by mutual written agreement.

1.2 Consultant shall assign a dedicated team lead who shall be available for at least 30 hours per week during the engagement period.

1.3 Client may request additional services beyond the scope of Exhibit A. Such requests shall be subject to a separate Statement of Work ("SOW") and additional fees.

2. TERM AND TERMINATION

2.1 This Agreement shall commence on February 1, 2025, and continue for a period of eighteen (18) months ("Initial Term"), unless terminated earlier in accordance with this Section.

2.2 Either party may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon sixty (60) days' prior written notice.

2.3 Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other party: (a) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within fifteen (15) days after notice; (b) becomes insolvent or files for bankruptcy.

2.4 Upon termination, Client shall pay Consultant for all Services performed through the effective date of termination, plus any pre-approved expenses. However, Consultant shall not be entitled to any termination fee, lost profits, or consequential damages arising from early termination.

3. COMPENSATION

3.1 Client shall pay Consultant a monthly retainer of $45,000 USD, payable within thirty (30) days of receipt of invoice.

3.2 For additional services under separate SOWs, Consultant's standard hourly rates shall apply: Senior Consultant: $350/hour; Associate Consultant: $225/hour; Analyst: $150/hour.

3.3 Consultant shall be reimbursed for reasonable travel and out-of-pocket expenses pre-approved by Client, not to exceed $5,000 per month without prior written consent.

3.4 Late payments shall accrue interest at a rate of 1.5% per month (18% per annum) on the unpaid balance. Client acknowledges that Consultant may suspend Services if any invoice remains unpaid for more than forty-five (45) days.

3.5 The Client shall bear all costs associated with any scope changes requested by Client, including but not limited to additional personnel, extended timelines, and third-party vendor fees, without any cap on such costs.

4. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

4.1 All work product, deliverables, reports, analyses, code, documentation, and materials created by Consultant specifically for Client under this Agreement ("Work Product") shall be owned exclusively by Client upon full payment.

4.2 Consultant retains all rights to its pre-existing intellectual property, methodologies, frameworks, tools, and know-how ("Consultant IP"), including any improvements thereto made during the engagement. Client is granted a non-exclusive, non-transferable, perpetual license to use Consultant IP solely as embedded in the Work Product.

4.3 Consultant may use generalized knowledge, skills, experience, and ideas acquired during the performance of Services, including for other clients, provided no Client Confidential Information is disclosed.

4.4 Client hereby grants Consultant an irrevocable, worldwide, royalty-free license to use, reproduce, and create derivative works from any Client data, systems architecture documentation, or technical specifications provided to Consultant during the engagement, solely for Consultant's internal research, training, and marketing purposes.

5. CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 Each party agrees to hold in strict confidence all Confidential Information of the other party. "Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by one party to the other, whether oral, written, or electronic.

5.2 The receiving party shall not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the disclosing party's prior written consent, except to employees and subcontractors who have a need to know and are bound by confidentiality obligations.

5.3 Confidentiality obligations shall survive termination for a period of five (5) years.

5.4 Notwithstanding the foregoing, Consultant may disclose the existence of this engagement and Client's name in its marketing materials, client lists, and case studies without Client's prior consent.

6. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

6.1 IN NO EVENT SHALL CONSULTANT'S TOTAL LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT EXCEED THE TOTAL FEES PAID BY CLIENT TO CONSULTANT IN THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM.

6.2 IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, SPECIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOST PROFITS, LOST DATA, OR BUSINESS INTERRUPTION.

6.3 Consultant shall not be liable for any damages or losses arising from: (a) Client's failure to implement Consultant's recommendations; (b) actions of Client's employees or third-party vendors; (c) changes in applicable laws or regulations; or (d) force majeure events.

6.4 CLIENT ACKNOWLEDGES THAT THE SERVICES ARE ADVISORY IN NATURE AND THAT CONSULTANT MAKES NO GUARANTEES REGARDING SPECIFIC OUTCOMES, COST SAVINGS, OR PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENTS.

7. INDEMNIFICATION

7.1 Consultant shall indemnify Client against third-party claims arising from Consultant's gross negligence or willful misconduct.

7.2 Client shall indemnify Consultant against all third-party claims, liabilities, damages, and expenses arising from or related to: (a) Client's use of the Work Product; (b) Client's business operations; (c) any regulatory investigations; (d) any claims by Client's customers or partners. Client's indemnification obligation shall be uncapped and shall survive termination indefinitely.

8. NON-SOLICITATION

8.1 During the term and for twenty-four (24) months following termination, Client shall not directly or indirectly solicit, hire, or engage any of Consultant's employees, subcontractors, or affiliates who performed Services under this Agreement.

8.2 Violation of Section 8.1 shall result in liquidated damages equal to one hundred fifty percent (150%) of the individual's annual compensation at Consultant.

9. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

9.1 Any dispute arising under this Agreement shall be resolved exclusively by binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association in New York, New York, under its Commercial Arbitration Rules.

9.2 Each party shall bear its own arbitration costs and attorneys' fees, regardless of outcome.

9.3 The arbitrator's decision shall be final and binding, and judgment may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction.

10. GOVERNING LAW

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of New York, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

11. GENERAL PROVISIONS

11.1 This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, and agreements.

11.2 This Agreement may be amended only by written instrument signed by both parties.

11.3 Consultant may assign this Agreement or subcontract any portion of the Services without Client's consent.

11.4 Any notice under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered by certified mail or email to the addresses set forth above.
